Examples and Definition of Percentage Increase

The difference between the end value and the starting value is expressed as a percent increase as a function of the starting value. The result of multiplying this decimal by 100 is a percentage increase between the final and starting values. The applications of percentage increase are extremely diverse. Statisticians may use it to gauge the likelihood of events, investors may use it to gauge returns, and accountants may use it to compute taxes.
